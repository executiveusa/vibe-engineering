# Public-Facing Release Gate

## Founder decision

No client-facing or public-facing deployment in the first five client lanes may be treated as ready for public release solely because builds, tests, deployment checks, or agent reviews pass.

Public release requires collaborative founder/cofounder review and explicit agreement that the experience meets PAULI STUDIO standards.

## Applies to

- MACS / MAXX suite
- Asc3nd
- Kupuri / Synthia
- Verified Vallarta
- Lane 5 when selected

## Required review dimensions

Before public-facing release, review the system together across:

1. Positioning and offer clarity
2. Site architecture and navigation
3. Visual design and taste
4. Copy and language quality
5. Blog/editorial system and current content
6. Bilingual/localization quality where applicable
7. Mobile experience
8. Accessibility and usability
9. Core conversion or engagement journey
10. Trust, proof, privacy, ownership, and operational clarity
11. Production evidence and rollback readiness
12. Commercial outcome alignment

Passing automated gates is necessary but not sufficient.

## Release states

- `TECHNICALLY_READY` — automated and engineering gates pass.
- `PUBLIC_REVIEW_REQUIRED` — technically ready but founder/cofounder experience review is incomplete.
- `PUBLIC_APPROVED` — both founder and digital cofounder agree the public-facing system meets the required standard.
- `SHIP` — public deployment is permitted after all required gates pass.
- `HOLD` — design, copy, content, product, evidence, or business issues remain.

## Collaboration rule

For each client lane, the audit must produce a public-facing review list before deployment. Design, copy, blog/content, and customer-facing journeys are reviewed together rather than delegated entirely to autonomous agents.

Agents may propose, implement, test, and prepare preview deployments. They may not interpret technical success as permission to publish a client-facing experience that has not received `PUBLIC_APPROVED` status.

## Verified Vallarta success target

Commercial success target for the first 30–60 days:

One verified local business becomes a paying PAULI/Kupuri client because of the Verified Vallarta ecosystem, with the source of the relationship tracked end-to-end.

Required chain:

`verified/listed business → sees value or receives useful visibility/lead → accepts paid microsite, lead-generation, or AI-enabled service → payment collected → source attributed to Verified Vallarta`

### Additional founder requirement

Before that commercial test is pushed publicly, Verified Vallarta's site design and blog/editorial experience must be reviewed and upgraded together. The current public experience is not assumed to meet the PAULI STUDIO quality bar merely because the deployment is healthy.

## Reasoning

The studio is building systems that represent real clients and brands. Automated engineering quality cannot judge taste, positioning, editorial credibility, cultural fit, or whether the experience feels ready to a prospective customer. Human judgment remains a release gate for all public-facing work while the factory handles repeatable technical execution underneath it.
