# Meeting OS Grill Follow-up

1. Read the meeting record, bead ID, attachments/transcript references, current project strategy, and open work.
2. Do not ask anything already answered in the record or recoverable from project files.
3. Build a decision tree of unresolved gaps that materially affect strategy, scope, capacity, ownership, deadline, public claims, safety/privacy, or implementation.
4. Ask exactly one question at a time.
5. For each question, include your recommended answer and why.
6. Resolve dependencies before downstream questions.
7. Stop when shared understanding is sufficient to produce confirmed facts, decisions, unknowns, strategy-update candidates, bounded Beads/tickets, and artifacts.
8. Never silently mutate canonical strategy. Produce a proposed strategy patch citing the source bead.

This adapts the decision-tree discipline of Matt Pocock's `grill-me` skill to persisted meeting evidence.
